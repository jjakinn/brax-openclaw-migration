## One-Sentence Summary

How a feud between Russian mathematicians Pavel Nekrasov and Andrey Markov led to the invention of Markov chains—one of the most important mathematical tools for modeling random processes, now used in everything from Google search to AI language models.

---

## The Feud (1905 Russia)

**Pavel Nekrasov** (Tsarist side):
- Religious, conservative
- Believed math could prove free will and God's existence
- Supported the Tsar during the 1905 revolution

**Andrey Markov** (Socialist side):
- Atheist, rigorous mathematician
- Criticized Nekrasov's work as "abuses of mathematics"
- Wanted math to be pure, not theological

---

## The Mathematical Innovation

**Law of Large Numbers:** As you flip a coin more times, the ratio approaches 50/50.

**Markov's Breakthrough:** What if events are NOT independent? What if the next state depends only on the current state?

This created **Markov chains**—systems where:
- You have a set of states
- Transition probabilities between states
- The next state depends ONLY on the current state (memoryless)

---

## Modern Applications

- **Google Search** (PageRank algorithm)
- **AI Language Models** (predicting next words)
- **Shuffling cards** (how many shuffles to randomize)
- **Physics simulations**
- **Speech recognition**

---

## Key Insight

Markov chains are everywhere because they model reality: we don't need full history to predict the future—often just the present state is enough.
