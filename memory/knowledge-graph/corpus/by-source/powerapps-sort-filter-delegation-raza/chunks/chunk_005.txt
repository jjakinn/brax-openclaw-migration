CHUNK 5: SORTBYCOLUMNS FOR MULTIPLE COLUMNS
Source: powerapps-sort-filter-delegation-raza | Timestamp: 03:30 - 04:30

Apart from sort, there is also a SortByColumns formula.

So what's the difference? With SortByColumns, I can sort on multiple columns.

---

Sort based on due date in descending order. Two items here where the sort date is 30th of November. Now the second sort I want to perform on the title in ascending order.

---

Comma title, `SortDirection.Ascending`.

The two items for 30th November. The second sort is on the title column.

---

With SortByColumns you cannot type in expressions. You simply pick columns. You cannot pick columns that are of type complex.

---

KEY POINTS:
- SortByColumns: Multiple column sorting
- Syntax: `SortByColumns(DataSource, Column1, Order1, Column2, Order2, ...)`
- Limitations:
  - No expressions allowed
  - No complex type columns
- Example: `SortByColumns(WorkTracker, "DueDate", Descending, "Title", Ascending)`
